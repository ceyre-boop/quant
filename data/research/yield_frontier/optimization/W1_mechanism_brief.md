## Mechanism stack (ranked)
1. **Attention-induced retail overpricing at the open → same-day reversal** [HIGH] — Berkman/Koch/
   Tuttle/Zhang 2012 JFQA is nearly our exact setup; Barber-Odean 2008 + Da/Engelberg/Gao 2011 supply
   the demand microfoundation; Boehmer et al. 2021 gives the retail-imbalance measurement tool.
2. **Lottery/MAX overpricing as immediate OVERREACTION** [HIGH] — Bali/Cakici/Whitelaw 2011; the
   overreaction refinement predicts the fade LIVES in unreliable/no-news moves and weakens/flips on
   hard catalysts (earnings/FDA) — the single most powerful conditioning variable.
3. **Liquidity-provision reversal** [HIGH mechanism / MED share] — scales with imbalance, inverse
   float, VIX; the layer most exposed to decay.
4. **Dilution machinery as the structural seller** [MED, most durable] — ATM/shelf takedowns into
   strength (DeAngelo et al. 2010); shelf-registered cash-burners should fade harder. Testable via
   S-3/ATM tagging.
5. Pump/promotion lifecycle [MED, subsample]; Lou/Polk/Skouras clientele tug-of-war as framing [HIGH].
**Why the edge survives:** shorting constraints (SSR, HTB) keep the overpricing unarbitraged —
Stambaugh/Yu/Yuan arbitrage asymmetry; decay weakest in illiquid high-idio-vol names (McLean-Pontiff).
